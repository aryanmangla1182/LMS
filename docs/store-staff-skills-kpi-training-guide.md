# Store Staff Skills & KPI Training Guide

## Purpose of this Document

This version is designed specifically to create 1-minute training videos per topic. Each section includes:

- Simple explanation
- Real-life scenario
- What good vs bad looks like
- Actionable steps
- Script-ready narration

## Core Skills

### 1. Adaptability & Problem Solving

Simple meaning:
Things do not always go as planned in a store. Adaptability means staying calm and finding solutions quickly.

Real scenario:
Customer: "I want this in size M."
Staff: "Sorry, not available."
Lost sale.

Better approach:
Staff: "Let me check alternatives for you. We have a similar style or I can arrange it from another store."
Sale saved.

Good vs bad:
- Bad: Saying "not available" and stopping
- Good: Offering 2-3 alternatives

How to improve:
- Always think: "What can I do?"
- Learn product substitutes
- Stay calm under pressure

1-minute video script:
In retail, problems happen every day: sizes missing, long queues, and unhappy customers. What matters is how you respond. Instead of saying no, always offer a solution. Suggest alternatives, check other stores, or recommend similar products. Customers do not expect perfection. They expect effort. A problem solved well can create a loyal customer.

### 2. Communication & Empathy

Simple meaning:
Good communication is not just talking. It is understanding how the customer feels.

Real scenario:
Customer: "I have been waiting for 10 minutes."

Bad:
"We are busy. Please wait."

Good:
"I am really sorry for the wait. Let me help you right away."

Good vs bad:
- Bad: Defensive tone
- Good: Acknowledge and reassure

How to improve:
- Listen fully before responding
- Use polite tone and body language
- Show understanding first, solution later

1-minute video script:
Customers may forget what you say, but they will remember how you made them feel. Always listen carefully. If a customer is upset, do not argue. Acknowledge their concern. A simple "I understand" or "I am sorry" can calm the situation instantly. Good communication builds trust, and trust drives sales.

### 3. Customer Focus & Service Excellence

Simple meaning:
Put the customer at the center of everything you do.

Real scenario:
Customer is browsing silently.

Bad: Ignore them.
Good: "Hi, are you looking for something specific or just exploring?"

Good vs bad:
- Bad: Passive service
- Good: Proactive engagement

How to improve:
- Ask questions
- Suggest products based on need
- Follow through till purchase

1-minute video script:
Great service is not about waiting. It is about anticipating. Greet every customer, ask simple questions, and guide them. When you understand their need, you can recommend better. Customers do not just buy products. They buy experiences.

### 4. Initiative & Drive for Results

Simple meaning:
Do not wait to be told. Take action.

Real scenario:
Store is empty.

Bad: Standing idle.
Good: Rearranging products, checking stock, and engaging walk-ins.

Good vs bad:
- Bad: Waiting for instructions
- Good: Taking ownership

How to improve:
- Set daily personal targets
- Track your performance
- Look for opportunities

1-minute video script:
Top performers do not wait. They act. Whether it is engaging a customer, fixing displays, or pushing sales, initiative makes the difference. Every small action adds up to big results.

### 5. Leadership & People Development

Simple meaning:
Leadership is helping others grow, not just managing them.

Real scenario:
New staff struggling.

Bad: Ignoring.
Good: Coaching and guiding.

Good vs bad:
- Bad: "Figure it out yourself"
- Good: Teaching and supporting

How to improve:
- Share knowledge
- Give feedback
- Lead by example

1-minute video script:
Leadership is not about position. It is about impact. Help your teammates, share what you know, and lead through actions. When your team grows, your store grows.

### 6. Teamwork & Collaboration

Simple meaning:
Work together to win together.

Real scenario:
Rush hour situation.

Bad: Everyone works separately.
Good: Clear role division across billing, floor, and stock.

Good vs bad:
- Bad: Blame culture
- Good: Support each other

How to improve:
- Communicate clearly
- Help teammates proactively
- Stay aligned on goals

1-minute video script:
Retail is a team sport. During busy hours, teamwork is everything. Support each other, communicate clearly, and focus on the customer. A strong team creates a smooth experience.

## KPI Training

### 1. Units Per Transaction (UPT)

Simple meaning:
How many items a customer buys in one bill.

Real scenario:
Customer buys a T-shirt.

Bad: Only billing.
Good: Suggest jeans or accessories.

How to improve:
- Recommend add-ons
- Create combos

1-minute video script:
UPT is all about adding more items to a single purchase. If a customer buys one product, suggest something that goes with it. Small additions increase sales significantly.

### 2. Average Transaction Value (ATV)

Simple meaning:
How much money each customer spends.

Real scenario:
Customer chooses a cheaper option.

Good:
Explain the benefits of the premium product.

How to improve:
- Upsell higher-value items
- Highlight quality and benefits

1-minute video script:
ATV increases when customers buy better products. Instead of focusing on price, explain value: durability, quality, and benefits.

### 3. Average Selling Price (ASP)

Simple meaning:
Average price per item sold.

How to improve:
- Push premium products
- Avoid over-discounting

1-minute video script:
ASP improves when you sell higher-value products. Always position premium options as better choices, not expensive ones.

### 4. Conversion Rate

Simple meaning:
How many visitors actually buy.

Real scenario:
Customer walks in and leaves.

Bad: No interaction.
Good: Engage and guide.

How to improve:
- Greet quickly
- Ask questions
- Assist actively

1-minute video script:
Conversion is about turning visitors into buyers. The faster you engage and understand the customer, the higher your chances of closing the sale.

## Final Integration Script

Great stores are built by great people. When you communicate well, understand customers, take initiative, and work as a team, your KPIs automatically improve. More items per bill, higher value sales, and better conversions all come from strong skills. Focus on the customer, and the numbers will follow.
